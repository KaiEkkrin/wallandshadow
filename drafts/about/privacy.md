# Privacy Notice

_What personal data Wall & Shadow collects, why, and your rights over it._

**Last updated: [DATE — fill in at publish]**

## 1. Who is responsible for your data

Wall & Shadow is operated by Alex Holloway, an individual based in the United
Kingdom. For the purposes of UK data protection law, Alex Holloway is the
"controller" of the personal data described in this notice.

You can contact us about privacy and your personal data at
**info@wallandshadow.com**.

## 2. What this notice covers

This notice covers the personal data we process when you use the Wall & Shadow
website and application at wallandshadow.com. It does not cover other websites
we may link to.

## 3. What personal data we collect

- **Account information** — your email address, your display name, whether your
  email address is verified, and your account tier.
- **Identity-provider information** — when you sign in through Zitadel (our
  identity provider), we receive and store an identifier (a "subject") that
  links your Wall & Shadow account to your Zitadel login.
- **Content you create** — the adventures, maps, game state and map changes,
  spritesheets, and invitations you create, including any names and descriptions
  you type.
- **Images you upload** — if your account is enabled for uploads, the images you
  upload, such as tokens, map and adventure backgrounds, and spritesheet images.
- **Technical information** — your IP address and standard request information,
  recorded in our server logs.

## 4. How we use your data, and our lawful basis

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

- **To provide the service** — creating and managing your account, storing your
  adventures, maps, and images, and showing them to you and the players you
  invite, including real-time play.
  _Lawful basis: performance of a contract — providing the service you have
  asked us for._
- **To keep the service secure and working** — preventing and investigating
  abuse, diagnosing faults, and keeping the service available.
  _Lawful basis: our legitimate interests in running and protecting a small
  service safely._
- **To understand overall usage** — we produce aggregate, anonymous statistics
  about how the service is used, by analysing our web server's access logs on
  our own server. We do not use analytics cookies, we do not track you across
  sites, and we do not use a third-party analytics provider.
  _Lawful basis: our legitimate interests in understanding whether the service
  is working and being used._

## 5. Cookies and local storage

Wall & Shadow uses only the storage in your browser that is strictly necessary
to sign you in and keep you signed in. We do not use analytics, advertising, or
tracking cookies. This is why Wall & Shadow does not show a cookie-consent
banner — there is nothing to consent to beyond the storage the service cannot
work without.

## 6. Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not share it with advertisers or
data brokers.

We use the following service providers ("processors") to run Wall & Shadow:

- **Hetzner Online GmbH** — a company based in Germany that provides our hosting
  and our S3-compatible object storage. Your account data, content, and uploaded
  images are stored on Hetzner infrastructure in Finland (see section 7).
- **Zitadel** — provides our identity (sign-in) service. Zitadel handles your
  login and holds your sign-in credentials, and is operated by a company based in
  Switzerland (see section 7).

Other users of Wall & Shadow can see the content you deliberately share with
them — for example, the players you invite to an adventure can see that
adventure's maps and images.

We may also disclose information where we are required to by law, or to protect
the service, our users, or others — for example, when responding to a report of
illegal content.

## 7. Where your data is stored

Our hosting and image storage are provided by Hetzner and are located in
Finland, within the European Union.

Our identity provider, Zitadel, handles your sign-in data. Our Zitadel instance
stores its data in Zitadel's European Union region, and Zitadel is operated by a
company headquartered in Switzerland.

Both the European Union and Switzerland are recognised by the United Kingdom as
providing an adequate level of data protection. Your personal data is therefore
not transferred outside jurisdictions that UK law treats as adequate, and no
additional transfer safeguards are required.

## 8. How long we keep your data

- **Account and content data** — kept for as long as your account is active.
- **When you delete your account** — your account information is deleted, and
  the adventures, maps, images, and other content you own are deleted with it.
  Where you have contributed changes to a shared map, those changes may remain
  as part of that map's history, but the link to your account is removed, so
  they are no longer associated with you.
- **Server logs** — access logs are kept for **[PLACEHOLDER — recommended: 30
  days]** and then deleted.
- **Invitations** — expire automatically.

## 9. Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

- ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you (access);
- ask us to correct inaccurate data (rectification);
- ask us to delete your data (erasure);
- ask us to restrict how we use your data;
- object to our use of your data; and
- ask us to provide your data in a portable form (portability).

The quickest way to have your account data erased is to delete your account in
the application. For any other request, contact us at **info@wallandshadow.com**.
We will respond within the time limits set by law.

## 10. Children's data

Wall & Shadow is intended for adults. You must be at least 18 to use it, and the
service is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal data
from children. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us
at **info@wallandshadow.com**.

## 11. Automated decision-making

We do not carry out automated decision-making that produces legal effects, or
similarly significant effects, concerning you.

## 12. How to complain

If you are concerned about how we handle your personal data, please contact us
first at **info@wallandshadow.com**, so we can try to put things right.

You also have the right to complain to the UK's data protection regulator, the
Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), at **ico.org.uk**.

## 13. Keeping your data secure

We take reasonable steps to protect your data. For example, uploaded images are
private and are served through short-lived, signed links rather than public
URLs, and access to content is controlled by account permissions. No service can
promise perfect security, and as a small project we cannot guarantee it — but we
take it seriously.

## 14. Changes to this notice

We may update this notice from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top
shows when it last changed.
